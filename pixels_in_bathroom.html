Pixels in Bathroom (2015)

Pixels in Bathroom (2016) was a photo sculpture installation in a digital media lab bathroom in Wesleyan University (Middletown, CT, USA), in response to a prompt to make a site-specific installation in my sculpture class. It consisted of stock photos printed in glossy photo paper, some of them folded into cubes that mimicked pixels.

Digital media lab is a building where students come to learn creative technology tools like photoshop, photo printing, 3d printing, laser cutting, and more. I remember spending late nights there staring at the pixelated screens of the computers as I worked on digital art project. In this project, I wondered what it would be like if the pixels came to life, specifically in the context of the bathroom.

I imagined bubbles falling out of the soap container, a sky falling from the window, goldfish swimming out of the toilet, and trees coming out of the toilet paper holder. In response, I found highly saturated free stock photo images and started printing them onto photo papers.

While doing so, I explored the meaning of "stock" in stock photos by creating cubes representing a cubed visual capital that can easily be transferred into a space. At the same time, I wanted to connect these cubes to both the external world and other cubes by using a flat 2d versions of the images.
