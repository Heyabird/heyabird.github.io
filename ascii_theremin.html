ASCII Theremin (2023)

This virtual theremin allows users to interact with an ASCII videoscape, playing sounds through the position and movement of their index fingers. Users can intentionally or accidentally a series of sounds by touching specific coordinates on the frame, resulting in a dynamic auditory experience.

The theremin utilizes web-downloaded samples related to the ideas of past, present, and future. Sliding one’s finger through the left border triggers samples sourced from royalty-free stock sound libraries using keywords like “retro” and “vintage” (e.g., cassette tape insert, arcade game sound effects, old windows computer sounds, etc.). Sliding through the right border produces sounds associated with the “future” (e.g. futuristic corporate logo effects, plasma gun sounds, rocket launch).

Waving the finger in the middle of the frame evokes familiar sounds of modern technologies, such as iPhone sounds, mouse clicks, and keyboard type noises, depending on the movement speed. Other surprise sounds (chimes, piano keys, drums, “moos”, “meows”, and more) are hidden for your pleasure.

This project was created as a final project for the School For Poetic Computation (SFPC)'s Musical Web Fall 2023 cohort.

To fully experience the project, camera and Wi-Fi access is required, and a desktop browser is recommended.
