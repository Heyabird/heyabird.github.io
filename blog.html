On Staying Focused

July 24, 2021

If you know me, you might know me as a girl who likes to bounce around and try a lot things. You're not wrong. Ever since I finished high school, my goal has been to explore the world and myself as much as I can.

And so I did. I lived my life like I was getting flavor samples from Baskin Robbins. During the past 7 years, I worked a lot of different jobs [1], formed relationships with all sorts of people, acquired various random skills, and moved cities [2] quite frequently. Overall, it was fun living as a professional amateur.

But that came at a cost: I was missing out on depth. As a master of none, I got to know many things superficially but rarely something very intimately. I also felt like I was missing a strong focus in life, as my direction kept shifting every month, day, or, sometimes, hour. And I didn't like how that felt anymore.

So, more recently, I've been feeling this immense desire to stay focused and dig deep on a less number of things [3]. Whether it be an entity, skill, object, or experience, I want to take my time in getting to know it slowly and deeply.

I know that this will be challeging for me at times, as my curiosity (or greed?) kicks in and tells to keep switching flavors. I can let myself indulge [4] in that voice from time to time, but I will also remind myself that life is short, and I can't have everything.

Changing flavors every bite means that I'll never know the second layer of the thing I've already bitten. And some things are worth choosing and diving deep.

I guess I'm finally seeing the beauty of commitment, and I am here to stay. At least for a little while. ðŸ§ƒ
